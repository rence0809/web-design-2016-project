Paris

is one the most beautiful country to visit. It is also known as the city of lights and the city of love. What fascinated me about this place are the stunning architectures, art galleries, astonishing shopping centres and the boat tour along the river seine. There is so much to love about paris. The authenticity of the parissian foods and patisseries will surely give you a mouthwatering experience. If you fancy visiting a luxury bakery, make sure to go to laduree in champs elysees. Its one of the best.Of course dont miss out disneyland, its one of the most magical and enchanting place. Its the best place to spend time with your families and even your other half. You'll enjoy all the rides, the parade and the spectacular fireworks at night. In my opinion its one of the best show.


During our stay in paris me and my friends got a chance to visit versailles which is less than 30mins by train. I was memerize by the castle. The meticulous design and structures were absolutely amazing. The great hall of mirror will surely make your jaw drop.The prestigious garden and orangeries is a ideal place to explore and stroll around.Versailles is one the best city to visit too.My experience in france was absolutely memorable ans i will cherish every bits of it.

Eiffel Tower

The nearest train station going to the Eifell Tower is "Trocadero station". Getting to the Trocadero station you need to take the Yellow Green line 9 and Green line 6.If you are in the different line you need to tranfer to either Yellow Green line 9 or Green line 6. When you arrive in the Trocadero station theres a sign which way is the Eiffel Tower and if you are outside go to your left then you can see the Eiffel Tower.

Musée du Louvre

The nearest station going to the Musée du Louvre is "Palais Royal - Musée du Louvre station".Getting to the "Palais Royal - Musée du Louvre station" you need to get the Orange line 1 and Pink line 7.If you are in the different line you need to transfer to the Orange line 1 or Pink line 7 at any station until you arrive in the "Palais Royal - Musée du Louvre station".When you arrive in the "Palais Royal - Musée du Louvre station" theres a sign which way is the Musée du Louvre and if you are outside in the station you need to cross in the pedestrian to your right and you can see the entrance.TIP: If you are an European Citizen you are entitled a free entrance.

Château de Versailles

There's a lot of activities that you can do in Versailles Garden. You can rent a bicycle for 17 euro (prices may change) and cycle around the garden for 30 minutes. I prefer this one because you can wander and explore each hidden places at your own time. The stall for renting is situated beside the Grand Canal. There's segway as well but its kinda pricey and its tour guided. The garden is so huge so make sure you wear comfortable shoes and outfits.

Disneyland

Euro Disney Resort or rather known as Disneyland Paris, was opened to the public on 1992. From the early 2000s Disneyland became the number one tourist attraction in Europe. It hosts several entertainment and recreational venues, with 4,800 acres it contains several resort hotels and a shopping and dining complex and of course, a castle! It was the very first Disneyland experience for Jorence. We were like little kids enjoying our free pass for the day. There was so much to do so many ride to take and so many places to visit! It truly is a child’s dreamland! I have no idea how many rides we’ve been on, how many times I felt like I was weightless that I could fly. The adrenaline rush was overflowing until the very end of the day where we got to sit down and immense ourselves with the atmosphere. As we were waiting for the last show, 20 minutes of fireworks display! It felt like a fairy tale come true. I almost couldn’t take my eyes of Sleeping Beauty’s castle as the water projection was shown with fireworks on each side creating an awesome show! Our time there made us conclude that, Jorence will visit all the other Disneyland Parks in the future!

Arc de Triomphe de l'Étoile

It was located at the center place of Charles De Gaulle. It was build between 1806 and 1836 and it was honor for those who fought for france during napoleonic wars. The entrance fee going up to the top arc is €8 for adult and for student between 18-25 years old is €5. The only way to go up to the top is to take the 40 stairs. It was very tiring when me and my girlfriend went up but when we went down it's not as tiring when you go up. In the top of the arc you will see the Eiffel Tower. The view is very beautiful specially at night, you'll see the city of lights.
